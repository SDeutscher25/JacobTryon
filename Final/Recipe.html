Cabbage Cream Stew

Cabbage Cream Stew
Leblanc Curry
Beneath The Mask
Phan-site

Ingredients

  • 1 pack bacon
  • 1 onion
  • 2-3 large carrots
  • 1 large crown broccoli
  • 1 Napa cabbage
  • 1 large container mushrooms
  • 1 bunch green onions Bean sprouts

Cooking

1. Cut bacon into ~1” pieces and sear on high heat, stirring to keep it from sticking. Usually don’t need to add any oil but a little bit doesn’t hurt if you’re afraid of it scorching/burning.


2. Add onions, usually sliced/julienned. Sear with bacon till they start turning clear ish but not overcooked.


3. Start adding other in ingredients according to how long they hold need to cook as if you were stir frying them. Usually after the onions I add the carrots first, then the broccoli and the mushrooms and finally the cabbage.


4. When the cabbage is added, add water to around half of the pot, not covering the ingredients - the cabbage will cook waaaaay down.


5. Cover and let it sit for a little on mid/mid-high heat and let it simmer till the cabbage cooks down and give it a stir.


6. Add 1-2 blocks of the cream stew roux. How many depends on how strong and thick you want it. If you add more and it’s too strong, add a little water at a time and stir and cook and let it equal out.


7. As that is happening, add “topping” ingredients - bean sprouts, snap peas, green onions cut into lengths around the size of the sprouts/peas. These you don’t want to overcook, so always add them at the end. They give a nice crunch to the mix.


8. Continue stirring on around mid-low to mid-heat; if it is at desired thickness, let cool a bit before serving.


9. Serve over rice - preferred is Japanese medium-grain. On the side I often have cold soft tofu - this is a great compliment to the hot stew - usually around a half a pack per person per bowl/meal, but I can also down a whole pack myself.
